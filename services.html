Georgia Institute of Technology

2018 - 2020

Master of Science, Computer Science - Specializing in Interactive Intelligence

After spending a year working with both early stage startups as well as a large international company, I grew an appreciation for robust products that focus on providing the best tools possible, whether it be recommending movies to watch or helping me find a new pair of shoes. This lead me to explore further education focusing in studying the interaction between humans and technology, particularly in the field of artificial intelligence.

As we are already experiencing, society is at the point where humans are able to interact with technology on an entirely different level. Artificial intelligence agents such as Siri, Alexa, and Google allow us to complete entire task processes, such as scheduling events or ordering groceries within a matter of seconds. My specialization in interactive intelligence allows me to explore the design of human-technology interactions and dive deeper into the world of machine learning and data-driven user feedback.

University of Wisconsin Madison

2013 - 2017

Bachelor of Science, Computer Science

Originally intending to major in biomedical engineering, I soon became interested in software after taking a prerequisite programming class. As I took more and more classes that were focused on computer engineering and software development, I grew to enjoy the creative freedom computer science allows. Given the right knowledge and tools, we can essentially build any platform or technology to solve any problem we may be facing.
